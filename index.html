WSI Embeddings → Classification & Survival

A thermal-safe, leakage-aware pipeline that turns pre-extracted whole-slide image embeddings into honest predictions on small clinical cohorts. Two findings: a validated tumor-grade signal on TCGA-CHOL (AUC = 0.80, permutation p = 0.005), and a pan-cancer survival framework using pooled leave-one-participant-out C-index with bootstrap CIs — because 5-fold survival CV on n ≈ 50 produces noise, not signal.

Three headline findings

0.80
A real tumor-grade signal

On just 26 balanced CHOL slides, KNN-5 separates low- from high-grade tumors at AUC 0.80 — and a 200-shuffle permutation test puts it well outside the null (p = 0.005).

+0.34
WSI adds real survival value

On TCGA-ACC, imaging lifts the C-index from a near-chance age+sex baseline (0.51) to 0.84 (HR 6.9 per SD). LIHC, CESC, COAD and STAD also show genuine WSI gains.

LOPO
Honest small-cohort CV

Pooled leave-one-participant-out with a bootstrap CI replaces noisy 5-fold survival CV — exposing that the old "RSF 0.71 ± 0.22" was fold-split noise, not signal.

The cohort at a glance

1 608 patients with valid survival across eight TCGA cancer projects — 557 events, 1 051 censored. Survival is modeled per cancer so a model trained on one never predicts another.
